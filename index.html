About Myself

I am a PhD student at Laboratoire 3SR (Université Grenoble Alpes, France) studying fabric and strain localisation within granular materials, using x-ray tomography. I am specially interested on the interplay between fabric evolution and the emergence of shear bands, focusing on the relation between the micro behaviour at the grain scale, and the macroscopic response of the materials. I am performing my PhD under the supervision of Cino Viggiani, Edward Andò, Alessandro Tengatinni, and Jacques Desrues. During the last years, I have become one of the main developers of spam , an open-source python package for handling and analyzing 3D x-ray tomography images.
